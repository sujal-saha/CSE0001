## 🔒 Stay Safe Online: College Student Checklist

As you navigate campus life and digital spaces, staying "Cyber Safe" is as important as your grades. Use this checklist to protect your identity, data, and hard-earned money.

### 💸 Financial & UPI Safety (Critical)
* **The "PIN is for Paying" Rule:** Never enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money. If someone tells you to enter your PIN or scan a QR code to get a "refund," "scholarship," or "prize," it is a scam.
* **Verify the 'Collect' Request:** Scammers often send "Collect Requests" on apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, or Paytm, hoping you’ll click "Approve" without reading. Always decline any unexpected request from an unknown ID.

### 📱 Social & Account Security
* **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Turn on 2FA for your WhatsApp, Instagram, and Gmail. This ensures that even if someone steals your password, they can't get into your account without a code sent to your phone.
* **Beware of "Free" College Wi-Fi:** Avoid logging into your bank accounts or entering sensitive passwords while connected to public or unsecured canteen/hostel Wi-Fi. Use a VPN or your mobile data for sensitive tasks.
* **Logout from Shared Devices:** If you use a computer in the college library or a cyber café, always log out of your accounts and **never** click "Save Password" in the browser.

### 🛡️ Browsing & Downloads
* **Avoid "Cracked" Software & Free PDFs:** Downloading pirated software or clicking "Free Textbook PDF" links from unverified sites often installs malware or keyloggers that track your typing. Stick to official sources.
* **Spot the "Urgency" Trap:** If you receive a message (SMS/WhatsApp) saying your "SIM will be blocked" or "Electricity will be cut" unless you click a link, it’s a phishing attempt. Official agencies don't communicate this way.
* **Check the URL:** Before entering credentials on a college portal or scholarship site, ensure the URL starts with `https://` and check for slight misspellings (e.g., `g0ogle.com` instead of `google.com`).

---

### 🚨 If You Get Scammed: Act Fast!
The first **2 hours** (the "Golden Hour") are crucial for recovering lost money. Do not be embarrassed—report it immediately.

1.  **Call the National Helpline:** Dial **1930** (Available 24/7).
2.  **File an Official Complaint:** Visit the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal at **[cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)**.
3.  **Contact Your Bank:** Immediately ask them to freeze your account or block your cards.
